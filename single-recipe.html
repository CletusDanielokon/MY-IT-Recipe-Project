Fluffy Banana Pancakes

Start your morning with these naturally sweet, incredibly fluffy banana pancakes. This quick and easy breakfast classic is a fantastic way to use up overripe bananas, which add a rich depth of flavor and moisture directly into the batter. Made with a touch of warm cinnamon and real vanilla, these golden-brown pancakes boast a light, tender crumb. Stack them high, top with fresh banana slices, and drizzle generously with pure maple syrup for a perfect comforting weekend brunch.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : beef breakfast pancakes food

instructions

step 1

In a large mixing bowl, peel your overripe bananas and use a sturdy fork to mash them completely until smooth with no large lumps remaining. Crack the egg directly into the mashed bananas, then pour in the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Whisk everything together vigorously until the wet ingredients are completely combined.

step 2

In a separate smaller bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. Pour these dry ingredients into your wet banana mixture. Use a wooden spoon or spatula to gently fold the mixture together just until the flour disappears. Be careful not to overmix—a few small lumps in the batter are completely fine and help keep the pancakes fluffy.

step 3

Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly coat it with a little butter or cooking spray. Scoop roughly 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot pan for each pancake. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set and dry. Carefully flip with a spatula and cook the other side for an additional 2 minutes until beautifully golden brown. Serve hot!

ingredients

2 large Overripe Bananas (the more spots, the sweeter!)

1 cup All-Purpose Flour

1 large Egg

3/4 cup Whole Milk (or any milk of choice)

2 tablespoons Butter, melted

1 tablespoon Granulated Sugar or Honey

2 teaspoons Baking Powder

1/2 teaspoon Ground Cinnamon & 1 teaspoon Vanilla Extract

A pinch of Salt

tools

Non-Stick Frying Pan, Griddle, or Skillet

Two Mixing Bowls (for wet and dry ingredients)

Sturdy Fork (for mashing) & Whisk

Flat Spatula (for flipping)

Measuring Cups & Spoons
