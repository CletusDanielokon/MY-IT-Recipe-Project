Nigerian Vegetable Soup (Edikang Ikong)

Hailing from the rich culinary heritage of the Efik and Ibibio people, Edikang Ikong is Nigeria's premier, iron-rich vegetable soup. This highly nutritious delicacy relies on a traditional, waterless cooking method where a mountain of fresh, thinly sliced waterleaves and fluted pumpkin leaves (Ugwu) collapse into a decadent base of pure palm oil. Packed with an extravagant assortment of tender meats, shelled periwinkles, stockfish, and intense crayfish flavors, it is a thick, dry, and deeply satisfying soup best paired with pounded yam, fufu, or eba.

prep time

40 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Soup swallow edikang-ikong vegetable

instructions

step 1

Wash your chopped waterleaves and ugwu leaves thoroughly in separate bowls to remove any sand, place them in individual colanders, and leave them to drain completely for at least 30 minutes. It is absolutely critical that the vegetables are as dry as possible, as authentic Edikang Ikong relies solely on the natural moisture of the waterleaves to cook.

step 2

In a large pot, combine your seasoned beef, shaki, kpomo, and stockfish with a little chopped onion and your blended yellow scotch bonnet peppers. Cover tightly and steam the proteins in their own juices over medium heat until completely tender and the remaining liquid has reduced down to just a few tablespoons of rich, concentrated stock.

step 3

Add the shelled periwinkles, ground crayfish, seasoning cubes, and the entire quantity of palm oil to the pot. Stir and let it simmer for 5 minutes. Next, stir in the drained waterleaves and let them cook down for 3 minutes. Finally, fold in the shredded ugwu leaves. Stir vigorously to combine, adjust salt if needed, simmer on low heat for just 3 more minutes until the greens blend together, and remove from the heat immediately.

ingredients

4 cups fresh Ugwu (Fluted Pumpkin leaves), finely shredded

6 cups fresh Waterleaves, chopped

1.5 lbs assorted proteins (Beef, Shaki, Kpomo, and Cow Tripe)

1 cup Shelled Periwinkles (cleaned)

1 large head of Stockfish & 1 medium Smoked Fish

1 cup pure Red Palm Oil

1/2 cup ground Crayfish

3-4 yellow Scotch Bonnet peppers, blended

2 bouillon Seasoning Cubes

Salt to taste

tools

Large Cooking Pot with tight-fitting lid

Two Large Colanders (for separate vegetable draining)

Sharp Chef's Knife & Heavy Cutting Board

Wooden Stirring Spoon

Measuring Cups & Spoons
