Boiled Yam with Fried Egg Sauce

A classic and comforting staple, this dish features thick slices of white puna yam boiled until perfectly soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet. It is paired with a vibrant, savory egg sauce made from a base of sautéed red onions, fresh plum tomatoes, and spicy scotch bonnets. Whisked chicken eggs are gently poured into the rich tomato base, creating soft, delicious curds that coat the palate beautifully. It is the ultimate hearty breakfast or quick dinner.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : beef breakfast pancakes food

instructions

step 1

Peel the yam skin using a sharp knife, slice the tuber into round discs or wedges, and rinse them thoroughly in clean water. Place the pieces into a cooking pot, pour in enough water to submerge them, add a pinch of salt, and cover. Boil on medium-high heat for 15 to 20 minutes until a fork easily pierces the center.

step 2

While the yam cooks, heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Sauté your sliced onions and minced scotch bonnet peppers for about 2 minutes until fragrant. Add the chopped tomatoes and diced bell peppers, stir in your seasoning cube, curry powder, and a pinch of salt. Fry down for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates to the top.

step 3

Whisk your chicken eggs in a separate bowl with a tiny pinch of salt. Turn down the burner heat to low and pour the eggs evenly over the bubbling tomato mixture. Let it sit undisturbed for 1 minute so the eggs can set into nice, large chunks. Gently fold the sauce together, let it simmer for another 2 minutes until the eggs are completely cooked, and serve hot with your drained yam.

ingredients

1/2 tuber of fresh White Puna Yam

4 large Chicken Eggs

3 medium plum Tomatoes, chopped

1 medium Red Onion, sliced

1 sweet Bell Pepper (Tatashe), finely diced

1 Scotch Bonnet pepper (Rodo), minced

1/3 cup Vegetable Oil

1/2 teaspoon Curry Powder

1 chicken Seasoning Cube

Salt to taste

tools

Medium Cooking Pot (for the yam)

Non-Stick Frying Pan & Spatula

Sharp Chef's Knife & Cutting Board

Small Mixing Bowl & Whisk (or fork)

Colander (for draining the yam)
