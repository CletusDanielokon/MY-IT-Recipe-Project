Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

The undisputed king of West African party cuisine, Nigerian Jollof Rice is a smoky, deeply flavorful dish celebrated for its rich orange-red hue. Long-grain parboiled rice is simmered slowly in a heavily reduction-cooked blend of plum tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and onions fried in seasoned oil. Infused with aromatic curry, thyme, and bay leaves, the recipe relies on a tight foil seal to trap steam, building that classic, irresistible "party-style" smoky bottom crust.

prep time

50 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Soup swallow nigerian dinner

instructions

step 1

Wash your long-grain rice thoroughly with warm water to remove excess starch, parboil it for 8 to 10 minutes, rinse, and drain completely. While draining, blend your tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and half of the onions together until smooth, then pour the mixture into a pot and boil it down until it forms a thick paste.

step 2

Heat your vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the remaining sliced onions and tomato paste for 5 minutes, stirring constantly to remove the sour taste. Pour in your boiled-down pepper blend and fry everything together for 10 minutes until the oil separates to the top. Stir in your curry, thyme, bay leaves, seasoning cubes, and your rich chicken stock.

step 3

Add the drained parboiled rice to the pot and stir thoroughly until every grain is coated in the red sauce base (the liquid should be just level with the rice). Place a sheet of aluminum foil over the pot, then seal it tightly with the lid. Cook on low heat for 25 to 30 minutes, allowing the steam to cook the rice. Once done, stir in your butter and fresh onion rings, crank the heat up for 2 minutes to get that smoky aroma, and serve!

>

ingredients

3 cups Long-Grain Parboiled Rice

5 medium plum Tomatoes & 3 large Red Bell Peppers (Tatashe)

2 medium Scotch Bonnet peppers (Rodo)

2 large Red Onions (half sliced, half blended)

1/2 cup Tomato Paste (concentrate)

2 and 1/2 cups rich Chicken Stock

1/2 cup Vegetable Oil

1 tablespoon Curry Powder & 1 teaspoon Thyme

3 dried Bay Leaves & 1 tablespoon Butter

3 chicken Seasoning Cubes & salt to taste

tools

Deep, Heavy-Bottomed Cooking Pot with Lid

High-Speed Food Blender

Heavy-Duty Aluminum Foil (critical for steaming)

Wooden Stirring Spoon

Sharp Chef's Knife & Cutting Board

Colander & Measuring Tools
